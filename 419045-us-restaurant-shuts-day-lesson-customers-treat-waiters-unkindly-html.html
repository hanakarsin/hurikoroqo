US Restaurant Shuts Down for Day as Lesson to Customers Who Treat Waiters Unkindly

  • Two restaurant owners in the US closed their business for a day to treat their employees to a day of kindness and debrief
  • This is after some of them were hurt by rude customers who berated them and threatened to sue them
  • The owners shared the news on their Facebook page prompting netizens to apologise to their employees on behalf of the nasty customers

Restaurant owners have left people speechless after shutting down their restaurant for a day in solidarity with their employees who had to endure rude customers after they were fully re-opened.

Restaurant closes after customers make employees cry

The whole incident happened when rude customers verbally abused the employees of a farm-to-table restaurant on Cape Cod, making the owners shut down the restaurant as an act of kindness to their employees.

While some people hardly have patience when ordering food, the employees had their last strike when one customer berated the youngest employee, who politely told him that he could not make an order yet as the restaurant was not yet open.

Their story was carried on New York Times, which reported the employers dedicated the rest of the day to their staff who had from time to time put up with nasty customers.

Part of what they shared on Facebook read:

"As many of our guests and patrons treat us with kindness and understanding, there has been an astronomical influx daily of those that do not, swearing at us, threatening to sue, arguing and yelling at my staff, making team members cry. This is an unacceptable way to treat any human. So Chef Regina and I have decided to take the day and give the staff time to deep clean the restaurant, train, and treat my staff to a day of kindness."
