Alfred Mutua Says DP William Ruto Needs to Be Protected as President in Waiting

  • According to Governor Alfred Mutua, DP William Ruto is the second most powerful personality in the country and thus needs top-notch security
  • He says Ruto is the president in waiting should anything befall the president, and that is why he should be heavily guarded
  • However, Mutua poked holes at the extend of the security enjoyed by the deputy president after it emerged a total of 257 officers have been assigned to guard him and his properties

Machakos Governor Alfred Mutua has somehow come to the defence of Deputy President William Ruto over the latter's security detail that has lately raised eyebrows.

Mutua said it was in order for the deputy president to be assigned elite guards given the importance of the office he holds.

According to the governor, at any given time, Ruto is the president in waiting should anything befall President Uhuru Kenyatta.

"That said, there is the question of how much protection does one individual need. Ruto's 257 guards

While appearing before the National Assembly's Departmental Committee on Administration and National Security, Interior cabinet secretary Fred Matiang'i clarified that Ruto is well guarded and there was no cause of alarm.

This is after Ruto's allies lamented after his elite GSU security team was replaced by their counterparts from the Administration Police.

During the session on Wednesday, September 1, Matiang'i revealed that a total of 257 officers drawn from various disciplines had been deployed to guard the second in command alongside his private properties.

Of the 257, Matiang'i said 74 were drawn from the presidential escort team, five from the GSU and six from the DCI.

The remainder, as revealed by the CS, were deployed to Ruto's residences and properties based across the country.

Ruto's properties guarded by state officers
