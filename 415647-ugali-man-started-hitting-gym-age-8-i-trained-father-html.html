Ugali Man Says He Started Hitting the Gym at Age 8: &quot;I Trained with My Father&quot;

  • Fitness trainer Charles Odongo alias Ugali Man said he started training at a young age
  • Odongo's father was a boxer, and he would hit the gym with his old man
  • Odongo, who recently became a millionaire, worked as a bouncer at one point

Ugali Man, Kenya’s latest internet sensation, has opened up on how he became a gym instructor.

Speaking to Jalang'o, the fitness trainer whose real name is Charles Odongo said he started hitting the gym at a young age, courtesy of his father.

According to Odongo, his father was a boxer and worked out by lifting weights. Then a young boy, Odongo would work out with him, and he fell in love with the gym.

“My father was a boxer and worked for the Kenyan Railways Corporation at the Kaloleni station. He used to work out by lifting weights, and he introduced me when I was eight.

Odongo divulged that his interest in working out made him his father’s favourite child.

Ugali Man, the bouncer

After completing school, Odongo worked as a bouncer for a while but later opted to quit the line of work, citing its violent nature.

“A foreigner came to Kenya training fitness instructors, and I joined the programme. After training for six months, I got my certification.I worked in different places before eventually opening my gym,” he said.

Going Viral

Recounting when videos depicting him eating ugali went viral, Odongo said he was asleep after a workout session when numerous friends called him.

"They said Robert Alai had shared my video, and the rest is history.”

He also declared his love for ugali, saying he eats the meal for breakfast.

Millionaire

On June 15, Odongo officially became a millionaire after being gifted KSh 5 million by a Kenyan betting company.

In the handover ceremony attended by Jalang’o, Odongo was presented with a dummy check and a brand new car.

The betting firm also provided him with new gym equipment.
