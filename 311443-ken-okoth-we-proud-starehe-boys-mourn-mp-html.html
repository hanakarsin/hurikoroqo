Ken Okoth: We are proud of you, Starehe old boys mourn MP

- Ken Okoth died on Friday, July 26, after long battle with cancer

- Starehe old boys have mourned him as a representation to the country and the world what Starehe stands for

Starehe old boys have joined Kenyans to mourn sudden death of their colleague Kibra MP Ken Okoth.

The lawmaker died on Friday, July 26, at Nairobi Hospital after long battle with cancer.

Okoth was rushed to the hospital and admitted at the Intensive Care Unit on Thursday, July 25. He died shortly after doctors removed him from the life support machines upon his request.

In their condolence message the old boys praised the MP as one who possessed extraordinary leadership qualities that were inculcated at their school.

"Okoth in his life showed leadership in the Starehe way and we remain proud of his representation to the country and the world what Starehe stands for. We will miss his leadership, his brotherhood within our society and his leadership to his community and our country," said Old Starehian Society Chairman Raymond Rono.

The hardworking MP was born in Kibra’s Kisumu Ndogo slums in 1978 before their father abandoned the family, leaving his mother Angelina to bring them up as a single mum.

He managed to sit for his class eight exams and scored 613 out of 700 marks in the Kenya Certificate of Primary Education (KCPE).

He was called to Starehe Boys’ Centre, but the family could not manage to raise money for his school fees.

His outstanding performance in primary school saw him qualify for a four-year sponsorship from Save the Children Fund, a non governmental organisation.

He finished his studies and secured another scholarship to study in New York’s St. Lawrence University.
