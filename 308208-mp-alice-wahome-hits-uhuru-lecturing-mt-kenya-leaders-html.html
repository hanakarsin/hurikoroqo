Jubilee MP hits back at Uhuru for referring to Mt Kenya leaders as 'thugs'

- Wahome termed as unfortunate the president's remarks during Akorino annual conference

- She said leaders from Central Kenya region, herself included, were not beneficiaries of anyone

- Uhuru had issued a stern warning to Jubilee leaders engaging in early campaigns despite his directives

- The president in a rare rant called the MPs "thugs" and claimed they never helped him ascend to power

Kandara MP Alice Wahome has hit back at President Uhuru Kenyatta for lecturing Jubilee leaders from Central Kenya region over early 2022 campaigns.

The vocal MP perceived to be a member of the team Tanga Tanga outfit allied to Uhuru's deputy William Ruto, termed as unfortunate the president's remarks during the 2019 Akorino annual conference held at Kasarani Stadium, Nairobi, on Sunday, June 16.

Speaking on NTV on Monday morning, the legislator appeared to indicate leaders from the Mount Kenya region were not elected because they supported Uhuru's presidential bid.

"I do not think I am a beneficiary of anyone, and I think that goes for everyone else. It is unfortunate that the president can stand on a religious podium and speak such words to his people," she said.

READ ALSO: Gideon Moi urges William Ruto to hold his horses on 2022 politics, give Uhuru space to work

Uhuru had issued a stern warning to Jubilee leaders, especially those from his backyard who were defying his directive to stop early 2022 politics.

The evidently angry president while delivering his speech at the Akorino event described the ever politicking Jubilee leaders as "thugs" and "rubbish" and cautioned them not to take his silence for weakness.

“When they see me remain silent, they should not think it's because I feel threatened. I will find them wherever they are...If you are making headlines, do not think it bothers me... I even stopped reading newspapers and using Facebook because of these rubbish,” he said in Kikuyu.

READ ALSO: Jubilee leaders allied to Uhuru issue stern warning to Ruto, accuse DP of disrespecting president

Uhuru also reminded the politicians he was not elected president because they looked for votes on his behalf and added he was not their "kihii" (uncircumcised boy).

“These thugs that we elected are busy politicking...they should not think I am their kihii (uncircumcised boy), they did not make me president, they will not stop me from constructing roads. They didn’t look for votes on my behalf, I went looking for my votes. These rubbish should stop bothering me,” he said moments before he apologised to the Akorino faithfuls for the angry speech.

This was not the first time the head of state lashed out at leaders for endless politicking.

He has on a number of occasions called on leaders from both sides of the political divide to give room for development and service delivery to the people.

"Those who have chosen to keep politicking instead of serving the people should be ready to give way. There are many young Kenyans who are willing and ready to work if they felt they are not up to task," he said on January 31, 2019.
