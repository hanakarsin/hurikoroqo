Thika Road: Hundreds of Motorists Stuck for Hours as Police Officers Strictly Enforce Curfew

- As soon as the 8pm curfew began on Saturday, April 17, police officers mounted roadblocks on various roads in Nairobi

- Thika Road was the most affected as hundreds of motorists decried heavy traffic jam

- Many Kenyans were infuriated by the occurrence and said it was counterproductive in the war against COVID-19

Hundreds of commuters and motorists have been stuck on various Nairobi roads after police officers put up roadblocks on the evening of Saturday, April 17, to punish curfew violators.

Reports reaching TUKO.co.ke indicate law enforcers erected the roadblocks on roads such as Kangundo, Waiyaki Way and Thika Road, with the latter being the worst affected.

Many motorists and commuters shared videos of the incident on social media explaining the situation, especially on Thika Road. No movement was taking place even for essential workers or services such as ambulances.

Kangundo Road was also at a halt but has since been opened up, and the traffic is almost clearing up.

In Lang'ata, the men in blue are allowing the essential service providers to pass.

Musician Bahati's wife Diana shared a clip of the unfolding drama on Thika Road, saying the police officers had inhibited any movement.

The roadblocks were mounted at Kayole Junction, Wilson Airport, KWS to Galleria, Thika Road, Mwiki, Kasarani and, Junction Mall.

Others were at Shujaa Mall, Coptic, Elgeyo Road, and at Arboretum.

At 11.20pm, police officers removed the barricades on Thika Road on both lanes and allowed motorists to continue with their journey home.

Many Kenyans were infuriated by the conduct of the police officers.

"Compliance with necessary COVID-19 public health measures is not about torture as happening on Thika Road but about national dialogue and community engagement on protecting lives," he said.
