Education Officials Refute Claims Narok Principal Treks for 20km Daily, Summon Her

- Education Officials in Narok said the principal had invited trouble for them by sharing her plight with the media

- The officials faulted her for 'tarnishing' their name after speaking out

- According to them, the principal had exaggerated the distance she covered daily; they clarified she was doing nine kilometres and not 20 as was reported

Officials attached to the education ministry are now on a school principal's case after reports emerged that she treks a couple of kilometres to deliver the KCSE exam papers.

According to the reports, Magdalene Kimani, the principal of Sosiana High School, was said to have been walking for 20km daily to deliver the papers to her 18 candidates.

The school is located in Trans Mara East, Narok county.

While speaking to the Nation, the teacher said she had been walking along the muddy Murkan-Sosian road in the company of two police officers, sometimes her journey being inconvenienced by heavy rains.

"We pick the exams from the Trans Mara East sub-county headquarters at 6am. A police van then drops us off at Murkan. From there, we have to walk on foot because no vehicle can traverse past that point," the principal was quoted.

After her story went viral, education officials in Narok summoned the principal faulting her for sharing her plight with the media instead of engaging her relevant seniors.

The officials stated that Magdalene had not been authorised to speak to the media about the challenges she had been facing.

While trying to save face, the officials said the distance the teacher had been covering was about nine kilometres and not 20 as had earlier been reported.

"What you are doing is not right. She will have to write her report. She will have to explain her 20-kilometre trek from the point where the vehicle drops her. No one has a right to speak on behalf of the ministry. The only person who has a right is the county director of education," said Steve Gachie, Trans Mara East sub-county director of education.

The officials said they will be accompanying her to the school to ascertain the distance she claimed she had been walking.

The principal said she was transferred to the school in January 2021 and though her family was hesitant at first to agree with the transfer, she said she has never regretted being its principal.

Magdalene said her driving force happens to be the resilience with which learners pursue their education regardless of their poor backgrounds.
