Police Brutality: 10 Times Police Officers Have Been Linked to Killing of Civilians

  • The recent killing of two brothers in Kianjokama in Embu county opened the wounds of police brutality cases in Kenya
  • Samatha Pendo is another victim of police brutality after she succumbed to police inflicted injuries in 2017
  • On February 15 2016, two police officers were convicted and jailed for manslaughter regarding the killing of a teenager, Kwekwe Mwandaza

For the longest time now, police have been accused of ending the lives of innocent Kenyans either by brutalising them or through extrajudicial killings.

Several activists including human rights organisations have repetitively raised an alarm over the killings that are now seemingly the order of the day in the country.

Even though some officers involved in the killings have been prosecuted, a section of them has walked scot-free.

1. Kianjokoma brothers

The recent killing of two brothers in Kianjokama in Embu county opened the wounds of police brutality cases in Kenya.

Benson Njiru, 22, and his younger brother Emmanuel Mutura, 19, were found dead three days after being arrested for allegedly breaking COVID-19 rules.

During the arrest, a witness said he was the police assaulting them. They would later be found dead on Wednesday, August 4 with multiple head and rib injuries.

Police implicated in the heinous act have since been arrested.

2. Baby Pendo

Samatha Pendo is another victim of police brutality when chaos broke out in Kisumu after the announcement of presidential results in 2017.

Widely known as Baby Pendo, the girl succumbed to injuries after she was hit by a police officer who stormed their house at night during the post-election turmoil.

A post-mortem conducted on Pendo revealed she suffered severe injuries on the back of her head.

Investigations commenced on the matter and five police commanders, who were involved in the operations, were found to bear the highest responsibility in her murder.

3. Kwekwe Mwandaza

On February 15 2016, two police officers were convicted and jailed for manslaughter regarding the killing of a teenager, Kwekwe Mwandaza, in Kwale in 2014.

Kwekwe died of a gunshot wound to the head from a high-powered rifle and another shot to the chest after 13 police officers stormed her home in Maweu village.

In their defence, the officers said they went to Maweu during an operation to pursue criminals.

4. Kibabii University student

In another incident, a Kibabii University student was shot dead in Trans Nzioa county by police officers who were on an operation targeting illicit brew dens in the area.

Sebastian Eugene was reportedly shot more than twice on the night of Thursday, December 10, 2020, while outside their house in Namanjalala, Kwanza constituency.

Residents of Namanjalala market took to the streets to protests against police brutality and demanded justice for the family of the second-year student.

5. Yasin Moyo

On Tuesday, June 2, 2020, the Director of Public Prosecutions (DPP) Nordin Haji approved the arrest and prosecution of a police officer accused of shooting dead 13-year-old Yasin Moyo at the balcony of his home.

On the fateful day, Yasin Moyo was curiously watching the police patrol the streets from their balcony with his mother and siblings when Duncan Ndiema Ndiwah shot him.

6. Naivasha fisherman

In another incident, a police officer was sentenced to 20 years in prison by a High Court sitting in Naivasha for killing a fisherman.

The police constable Evans Maliachi Wiyema was in February 2021 found guilty of killing fisherman Moses Kinyanjui Wanyoike.

Wanyoike was reportedly fishing in Lake Naivasha, Nakuru county when he met his death.

7. Willie Kimani

The human rights lawyer Willie Kimani is said to have also been tortured and killed by the police.

Bodies of the 32-year-old Kimani, his client Josephat Mwenda and taxi driver Joseph Muiruri were found at Ol Donyo Sabuk River on Thursday, June 30, 2016.

Then inspector general of police Joseph Boinnet said there was circumstantial evidence linking three Administration Police officers to the deaths.

8. Ruaraka OCS beating inmate to death

In another incident, former Ruaraka Officer Commanding Station (OCS) was found guilty of torturing to death Martin Koome on the night of December 19, 2013.

While in prison, the court heard Koome was assaulted with a metal pipe and his head immersed in a drum of water.

The police officers who took him to Kenyatta National Hospital 20 hours later, said he had been beaten by a mob.

9. Police officers shoot 7 taxi drivers in Kawangware

Also, six Administration Police officers were on December 19, 2012, sentenced to death for killing seven taxi drivers in Kawangware, Nairobi county.

The officers were found guilty by a Nairobi court and convicted of seven counts of murder.

However, on June 28, 2014, the Court of Appeal acquitted the six saying they acted in self-defence.

10. Meru University student leader

In another instance, Meru University was engulfed in shock and disbelief after police shot dead Meru University Student Association (MUSA) Secretary-General Evans Njoroge, on Wednesday, February 27, 2018.

Witness alleged that police chased after him before killing him at close range, in what appeared to be a bullet to the head during student protests.
