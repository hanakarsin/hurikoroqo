Cliff Ombeta Says Poverty Levels in Bonchari Pushed Him to Seek MP Seat: &quot;We'll Not Lose&quot;

  • Cliff Ombeta declared his political ambition for the Bonchari parliamentary seat on June 30
  • The criminal lawyer said he would contest the seat on a United Democratic Alliance (UDA) party ticket
  • Poverty levels, cronyism and greediness of Bonchari politicians pushed him to the murky world of politics

Criminal lawyer Cliff Ombeta is a recognisable face in the corridors of justice, where he has sought justice for petty and capital offenders in equal zeal.

Ombeta, the managing director at Ombeta and Associates, on June 30, declared he would run for the Bonchari parliamentary seat in the 2022 General Election with the newly-formed UDA party.

For a man who shied from mainstream national politics, the announcement to plunge into the murky world of politics came as a shock for people who vied him as a successful city advocate.

Ombeta's new call

When TUKO.co.ke sought to find out why he thinks he is the best bet for the residents of the Bonchari constituency, Ombeta said it is time to go back to his roots and help fight poverty among his people.

In his own words, the crème de la crème lawyer disclosed he had been engaged in many charity work in many places other than his own area, and now, it is time.

"It is time to go back to my roots. Too much delay in development. My constituency has always lagged behind in development. Every time, we have been circulating the same people as MPs in Bonchari. Same old stories, same old positions. I need to give back to my own people," he responded.

Two things pushed him to try his hand in politics; cronyism and greed of past Bonchari legislators he claimed developed areas where they got most votes.

"Time to see everyone as equal. Time to assist improve our Bonchari economy. Time to see our schools improve. Time for the poor to have a chance in society and development. Hon Oroo (now late) had a vision and an agenda, unfortunately, he passed away before he cold fulfill," he added.

UDA Bonchari loss

The UDA party is still reeling from the humbling loss in the May 18 Bonchari by-election in which the Orange Democratic Movement candidate Pavel Oimeke beat Oroo Teresa Bitutu.

However, he attributed the loss to intimidation of their supporters by state agents and the police officers before and during the mini poll.

"We (UDA) will not lose. We fought against the state and county machinery, which unlike in the by-election will not be the case in the general election," he stated.
