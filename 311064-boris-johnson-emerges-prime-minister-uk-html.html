Boris Johnson wins race to be UK prime minister

- Boris Johnson defeated his rival, Jeremy Hunt, after a seven-week contest

- The winner polled 92,153 votes while Hunt managed 46,656 votes

- The new leader will be taking over from Theresa May effective Wednesday, July 24

The former mayor of London and popular historian Boris Johnson has won the race to be the new prime minister of the United Kingdom.

Boris, who was elected leader of the Conservatives on Tuesday, July 23, will be taking over from Theresa May effective Wednesday, July 24.

He defeated his rival, Jeremy Hunt, after a seven-week contest after which Boris polled 92,153 votes while Hunt polled 46,656 as reported by the Guardian UK

Boris will be amusing office at a time the United Kingdom is facing political crisis over Brexit.
