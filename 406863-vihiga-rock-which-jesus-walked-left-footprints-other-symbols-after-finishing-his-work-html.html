Vihiga Rock on Which Jesus Walked, Left Footprints and Other Symbols after Finishing His Work

- The prints are clearly engraved on the rock and resemble a human footprint on a freshly constructed cement floor

Tourism is a key income generation activity in Kenya that fetches significant foreign income.

Apart from wildlife, Kenya boasts of several beautiful and natural sceneries like the famous crying stone in Ilesi in Kakamega, Hot springs of Lake Bogoria, Kit Mikai caves in Siaya and The Thomson falls in Nyahururu, among others.

In Matsigulu village in Vihiga county, there are famous rocks that gained their popularity on the belief that they have Jesus’s footprints, Kenyan and African maps, and other symbolic prints.

If Jesus Walks as Kanye West sang, then the locals here believe it was not only on water but also on Gevera rocks in Matsigulu where he literally left a footprint en route back to heaven.

Matroba Otwere, 70, the owner of the land where the rock sits, told TUKO.co.ke that locals believe the boulder has some sacred secrets about God’s kingdom.

The locals and even people from far areas of Vihiga believe that Jesus appeared in Matsigulu village.

“It is what the people around here believe. Even my husband who died early aged 94 had no clear interpretations of the sign but the rocks are sacred,” Otwere stated.

Many famous Kenyans have visited the rock for adventure and sacred religious reasons.

First Lady Margaret Kenyatta is among those who visited the rocks that are also a tourist attraction site.

The widely spread flat rock occupies an approximate area of 70 metres and bears many symbols which the locals attribute to the work Jesus accomplished on earth.

Jesus's footprints

These prints are clearly engraved on the rock and resemble a human footprint on a freshly constructed cement floor.

The footprints are spread one stride apart and are facing in one direction, indicating a person walking.

A human sole measuring at least size 40 fits perfectly in this print.

“We believe that Jesus, after completing his work walked towards that direction before ascending into the clouds,” Otwere explained.

Bread and fish

Fish and bread apparently engraved on the rock and Otwere said this was what Jesus used to feed 5,000 hungry men.

The bread was also used as an emblem representing the body of Christ during the last supper, according to Otwere.

“Bread and fish are cited among meals Jesus used for his works in different biblical scenarios including the last supper,” she said.

These two items are also identified among engravings on the rock’s surface.

Some locals believe that the two were used for security reasons.

Florence Angara, another Matsigulu resident, alluded that the knife was used by Jesus’s disciple Peter to cut the ear of an officer that wanted to arrest Jesus.

The Kenyan and African map portraits

Even at a close view, one cannot easily note these two maps unless guided by the locals who identified the engravings and are familiar with what they resemble.

The two maps lie adjacent to one another facing in different directions.

In relation to these, Otwere said they revealed that God loves Kenya and Africa at large.

Also, at a distance view, the rock’s general shape depicts the shape of the African map.

“We believe that Jesus came to Africa. His presence was felt here too and that is why we have the two maps here,” she said.

Sheep and cow hoof prints

Small hoof-like potholes well engraved on the rock’s surface are also on the list of symbols locals believe tell the story of Jesus’s work on earth.

At a close examination, one may think that a cow and a sheep walked on the rock while still molten and the prints left to dry.

TUKO.co.ke learned from Angara that the sheep represented Jesus’s unmatched humility; despite being a king; he was humble during his mission.

There are also two paths of variable width lane laid beside each other believed to be trodden by Jesus during his visit to this village.

Numerical symbols and illegible inscriptions.

There sits another smaller rock inscribed with numbers 4, 5, 14, 17 and 27 alongside other illegible writings just some meter away from this rock.

Locals claim that no one knows the numbers' meanings but believe, just like the writing on the wall, time shall reveal.

Jesus’s tombstone with the number 121 on it

The most interesting part of this famous stone is Jesus’s tombstone.

The stone of about 6 feet long with a narrowing bottom end is believed to belong to Jesus and has been static in one place from time immemorial.

The locals also claim that no man or even machine can displace or shake this medium-sized stone from its position.

“In 1993, a young man in this village tried displacing this stone and after failing to do so he broke a piece from it. His stomach swelled and passed on shortly after,” Angara collectively narrated.

Unfulfilled promise

Otwere lamented that the Vihiga county administration had pledged to purchase the land around the rock and safeguard it for tourism purposes, but so far, the promise remains unfulfilled.

“Ex-governor Moses Akaranga promised to purchase this area and safeguard it for tourism and heritage purposes. He failed to do it, neither has his successor Wilbur Ottichilo done so,” Angara said.

Contrary to the locals’ claims, Ottichilo recently said his administration was focused on mapping and protecting tourist attraction sites.

“We have many historical and tourist attraction sites and as a government, we are working to secure and develop them,” he said.

Despite Jesus’s footprints on the rock having a spectacular and rare feature, the area is not yet secured, human activities are now eroding many symbols.

“We don’t charge any fee to those visiting the rock. Worshippers come a long way to quench their desires on this rock. It’s a blessing indeed,” Angara said.

She, however, said, if not protected, human activities would erode the amazing symbols and make the rock lose its aesthetic and religious value.
