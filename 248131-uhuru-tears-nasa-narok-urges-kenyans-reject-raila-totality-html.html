Uhuru tears NASA apart in Narok, urges Kenyans to reject Raila in totality

- President Uhuru Kenyatta and his deputy, William Ruto, took their campaigns to three counties on Saturday, July 29

- In Narok, Uhuru and Ruto attacked the opposition NASA and urged Kenyans to reject them on August 8

- Ruto said NASA is now blaming the military because they have sensed defeat in the August 8 polls

- Uhuru said NASA has no agenda for Kenyans but thirst for power

President Uhuru Kenyatta took his campaigns to three counties on Saturday, July 29, as he seeks re-election in the August 8 General Election that is just days away.

While addressing residents of Narok county at Nairagie Enkara, Uhuru told National Super Alliance (NASA) leader Raila Odinga to stop clutching at straws and tell Kenyans the plan he has for the country.

Uhuru dismissed the opposition saying they have nothing for Kenyans except excuses aimed at delaying and derailing the August 8 polls.

He said Raila should stop employing politics of threats and instead explain how he plans to build on the progress Kenya has achieved in the last four years.

“Our opponents have no credible agenda for Kenya. They cannot tell us they will deliver change when they failed to achieve anything meaningful when they were running the government,” Uhuru said.

He said unlike the opposition candidate, he has presented a credible action plan that has measurable objectives to create more jobs for the youth and lower prices for Kenyans.

His sentiments were echoed by Deputy President William Ruto who said Kenyans will have to choose between development and empty rhetoric.

Ruto added that NASA is now blaming the military because they know they cannot beat Jubilee in the polls.

“First it was the IEBC commissioners, then it was the BVR, then it was the presidential vote tallying. Now they are blaming the security forces,” he said.
