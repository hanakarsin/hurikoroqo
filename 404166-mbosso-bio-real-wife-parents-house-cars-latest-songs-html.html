Mbosso bio: real name, wife, parents, house, cars, latest songs

Mbosso is a young and extraordinarily talented musician who hails from the Republic of Tanzania. Since he rose to stardom, he has been releasing hit after hit. With time, he has become one of the best singers in East Africa. The crooner is known for songs such as Tamba, Nadekezwa, Hodari, and Watakubali. If you are his fan, you may find his biography thrilling and inspiring.

The music scene is among the most challenging fields in the entertainment industry. For instance, it is tough to remain relevant and consistent. Many artists prematurely end their careers because they cannot keep up with trends. However, that is not the case with Mbosso. Although he had a tough beginning, he has worked his way up the ladder to become a star.

Mbosso biography

What is Mbosso's real name? His birth name is Joseph Kulungi. He was born on 3rd October 1991, meaning he is 29 years old as of 2021. Mbosso was brought up by humble parents – his father was a conductor, while his mother was a small scale farmer.

Where is Mbosso from? The Bongo Flava star was born in Tanzania. He particularly spent most of his childhood in a place known as Kibiti, Rufiji in TZ. Growing in a humble background was an unpleasant experience for him. He was forced to work at a fast-food restaurant to help his family put food on the table.

Mbosso studied until form four. Unfortunately, he had to end it there because of his family background. Instead, he requested his parents to allow him to pursue his passion – music. He eventually managed to persuade them to leave for Dare Salam.

On arriving in Dare Salam, life was not easy. At some point, he contemplated going back to the village. After several days in the city, Mkubwa Fella bumped into him in the street while playing the guitar. He introduced him to his band, Wakubwa na Wanawe.

Career

He launched his music career with Wakubwa na Wanawe, which later became known as Yamoto Band. The band comprised three other equally gifted young singers, namely Enock Bella, Aslay, and Beka Flavour.

The band released a few tracks that performed extremely well. Nevertheless, the group was disbanded in 2018. It later emerged that the boys had issues among themselves that forced them to part ways. Some of them believed that they could do better on their own.

Mbosso left Dar es Salaam for Kibiti, his home, feeling disappointed. On arriving home, he went back to farming with his mother. Considering they had a small farm, it could not sustain all their needs. So, he started to engage in criminal activities.

House and cars

Mbosso now lives like any celebrity, driving a nice car and living in a decent house. The star unveiled his Toyota Land Cruiser Prado car to the public in early 2020. Previously, he was gifted another vehicle by the WCB Wasafi Record Label for his exemplary work.

In 2020, he also gifted his parents with a brand new car and house. He was brought up in a religious home. Perhaps, this helps to explain why he always puts God first in his life. On the day he handed the house to his parents, the Tamba crooner took to social media with the following message,

Mbosso net worth

Mbosso is now among the wealthiest musicians in TZ. The star has made a sizable amount of fortune since he made his music debut with WCB Wasafi. His songs are among the most played, downloaded and streamed on various music platforms.

He is the brand ambassador of Tanga Fresh milk. Some sources estimate his net worth to be around $70,000, which is roughly 162 million TZS. However, he is yet to come public about his wealth.

Is Mbosso married?

Currently, he is reportedly single. In 2019, mbosso's wife, Boss Martha, succumbed after a short illness. She was a regular comedienne on Cheka Tu. By the time of her death, the couple had a 4-year-old son. The star reportedly has another child with his girlfriend, Rukia Rucky.

Indeed, Mbosso has come a long way to become among the best Bongo Flava stars in TZ and East Africa. His passion for music has been driving him to go the extra mile to put a smile on the faces of his fans. Aside from his music career, he is a father of two. Overall, Mbosso inspires many upcoming artists to pursue their passions regardless of their backgrounds.
