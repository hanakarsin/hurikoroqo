The Tanzanian government has threatened to deal with dishonest civil servants who stole taxpayers' money in the National Health Insurance Fund (NHIF).

Health minister Dorothy Gwajima asked anyone who was implicated or played a role in the loss of KSh 97.7 million to benefit children under NHIF programmes, to return the loot.

“Those who participated in the theft are ordered to start returning the money and that there will be no mercy,” Gwajima said as quoted by The Citizen on Friday, July 2.

The minister said it was unfortunate that patients were being turned away or even forced to buy drugs elsewhere, which should have been provided freely by the government if there was no corruption at NHIF.

Tanzania launched the long-awaited NHIF insurance packages at the end of November 2019 at a time the government was striving to provide affordable healthcare.

The scheme covers Tanzanians of all ages regardless of where they work. It can be for the government, the private sector or self-employed.

NHIF has attractive services that it offers to its beneficiaries through accredited health facilities countrywide.

Beneficiaries are offered these services as per standard treatment guidelines issued by the fund's regulations alongside the Ministry of Health.

Despite the challenges, Gwajima stated that her ministry would come up with a KSh 6.7 billion health insurance strategy after the signing of its bill later in Parliament in September 2021.
