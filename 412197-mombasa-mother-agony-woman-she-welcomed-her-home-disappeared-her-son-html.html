Mombasa Mother in Agony after Woman She Welcomed in Her Home Disappeared with Her Son

- Three weeks ago, Tina Sammy, who sells wigs online, got a call from a woman who said she wanted a wig; this was the second time she was doing business with her

- The woman later appeared in her home together with a teenager believed to be her daughter to buy the product, and Tina soon realised they were not in a hurry to leave

- At around 6pm, the woman said she wanted to buy Tina's son some diapers before she left, and so she sent her daughter to the shop

- The teenage girl went to the shop with Tina's son, and soon enough, the woman also left the house, and they both disappeared with the nine-month-old baby

On April 26, 2021, Tina Sammy, who sells wigs, received a call from a woman she last saw in December 2020 when she last sold a wig to her.

The Mombasa woman sells wigs online, and so when the woman called her asking for the product, she did not smell anything suspicious as she thought her customer would visit her home, buy a wig and leave.

Speaking to TUKO.co.ke, Tina said when the woman appeared in her house, she was in the company of a teenage girl purported to be her daughter, and she soon realised that they were not in a hurry to leave because they complained of being a little tired.

"She said she had spent her day looking for me and so she would take her time selecting the wig of her choice. I did not have a problem because I had met her in 2020 when I was selling another wig to her," she recalled.

The mother and daughter had stayed in her house for around seven hours when the woman told Tina that she wanted to buy some diapers for her nine-month-old son Mohammad Taki.

The woman then sent the teenage girl to the shop to purchase the diapers for Tina's son. The young girl opted to go to the shop with the child, in what the wig-seller would later realise was part of the plan to kidnap Taki.

However, five minutes after the woman's daughter had gone to the shop with Tina's son, the baby was heard crying, and the customer told Tina she would check why the little boy was crying.

A moment after the woman checked on the baby outside; Tina immediately smelt a rat as she noticed the lady had left with her handbag.

"I rushed to the shop where the girl was to buy the diapers and found neither the woman, her daughter or my son. I then went back to my house to see if they had gone back but I did not find them and that was when it dawned on me that my boy had been kidnapped," the heartbroken mother said.

A CCTV footage showed the woman and her daughter hopped onto a motorbike as soon as they left the house with Tina's baby.

The distraught mother reported the matter to Bamburi and Kadzandani police stations.

On Saturday, May 15, Tina got hold of a Facebook account that she believes belongs to the kidnapper and shared her photos online.

Coincidentally, as soon as details and photos of the Facebook account started circulating widely on social media, the account was deactivated, further raising suspicion.

The woman is now clinging to hope that she will hold her son in her arms once again.
