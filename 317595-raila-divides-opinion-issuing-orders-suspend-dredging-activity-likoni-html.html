Raila divides opinion after issuing orders to suspend dredging activity in Likoni

- Raila was in Likoni to condole with family of Mariam Kigenda and her daughter Amanda Mutheu

- Miriam and Amanda have been missing for eight days since they drowned at Likoni Channel on September 26

- The AU envoy directed dredging work on the shores of the Indian Ocean be stopped to facilitate search mission

- He argued the dredging activity was interfering with ocean visibility and making it more difficult to find the victims

A heated debate has erupted following ODM leader Raila Odinga's orders to suspend dredging activity on the shores of the Indian Ocean amid intensified search for missing mother and child who drowned at Likoni Channel.

Whereas a section of Kenyans appeared to wonder in what capacity the former prime minister was issuing the orders, others had no issue with the directive.

Raila who was in Likoni, Mombasa county, on Saturday, October 5, to condole with family of Mariam Kigenda and her daughter Amanda Mutheu had directed the dredging activity on the shores of the Indian Ocean be suspended immediately to facilitate the recovery mission.

"I want to give an order here that the dredging works stop immediately so that those who are working here can work without interference," he ordered and further directed that stringent measures be put in place to avoid such a tragedy from ever taking place again.
