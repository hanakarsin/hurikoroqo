Sirisia MP John Waluke found guilty in maize graft case, remanded awaiting sentencing

- MP John Waluke was convicted alongside Grace Sarapay Wakhungu for KSh 297 million maize fraud case

- Magistrate Elizabeth Juma said the prosecution had proved its case beyond reasonable doubt and further ordered the lawmaker to be remanded

- The DPP said the case had taken less than two years and was among the first case Noordin Haji approved for prosecution

- The duo allegedly received the hefty cash purporting to be the cost of maize storage

Sirisia MP John Waluke has been found guilty of various corruption charges among them the KSh 297 million maize fraud case.

Magistrate Elizabeth Juma said the prosecution had proved its case without reasonable doubt and ordered the lawmaker to be remanded as he awaits sentencing on Thursday, June 25.

According to the Office of Directorate of Public Prosecution (ODPP), the case was among the first that Noordin Haji approved for prosecution in April 2018 when he took office.

Waluke was convicted alongside Grace Sarapay Wakhungu, the mother of former environment minister and now Ambassador Judy Wakhungu.

"The case has taken less than two years to conclude. This is among the first cases approved for prosecution by DPP Noordin Haji upon taking office in April 2018," a statement from ODPP read in part.

The legislator was also acquitted of two charges among them uttering a false document.

Waluke and Wakhungu, who are both named as directors of Erad Supplies and General Contracts Limited, were accused of fraudulently acquiring KSh 297 million purporting to be the cost of maize storage.

They also allegedly obtained a further KSh 13 million of public money on grounds of having offered storage services through Chelsea Freight and another KSh 24 million.

Waluke and Wakhungu allegedly committed the offense on separate dates between 2009 and 2013.
