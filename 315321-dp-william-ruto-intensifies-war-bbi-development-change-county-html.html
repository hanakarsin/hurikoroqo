DP William Ruto intensifies war against BBI, says only development will change county

- Ruto had earlier stated that the country's electoral problems were caused by Raila who never accepts poll results

- He asked the ODM leader to publicly state what he wanted done for him so that he learns how to concede to defeat

- On the other hand, Raila accuses the DP of instigating an early plot to succeed President Uhuru Kenyatta

- Raila takes issue with Ruto for opposing the BBI stating that the DP is opposing something that he even doesn't know its contents and recommendations

Deputy President William Ruto has continued to poke holes in the the Raila Odinga-backed Building Bridges Initiative (BBI) stating that push for constitutional amendment has no capacity transform the country.

Speaking in Godo, Kwale County on Saturday, September 14 during commissioning of Lunga Lunga Technical and Vocational College, Ruto maintained that it was only development implementation that would launch the country on a transformatory trajectory.

The DP also took the chance to take a swipe at his political nemesis who have constantly accused him of early 2022 politics.

In his defense, the second in command said his spate of tours in different places across the country are in line with his job description of deputy president.

"They keep on speaking that the deputy president is always moving around the country. They have constantly accused me of trying to shift the political ground in my favour. However, what they should know is that I am here in the capacity of deputy president. I am at work, this is the work of deputy president. I was not elected to just sit in the office, let them not plug politics in matters development," Ruto said.

According to Ruto time for politicking and campaigning had not come insisting that he will only indulge in Uhuru succession debate when time was ripe.

Leaders allied to ODM chief Raila and those who subscribe to Nominated MP Maina Kamanda led Team Kieleweke are among those who claim the DP and his Team Kieleweke are campaigning instead of helping President Uhuru Kenyatta run the country.

This accusation was recently presented before Uhuru by former Kiambu Governor William Kabogo during burial of Benga maestro John DeMathew.

Ruto, however, dismissed the claims urging the president to ignore them.

"Because Kabogo is equally your friend and mine too, he gets cheeky sometimes. He has now come to report me to you that I am involved in endless politics. I will, however, like to clear the air and tell you that the accusations are not true. If there are people involved in 2022 campaigns, then you can be sure I am not among them. That is not me. It is another clique of leaders," Ruto defended himself.
