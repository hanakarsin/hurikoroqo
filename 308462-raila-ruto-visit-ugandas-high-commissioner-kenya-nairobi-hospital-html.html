Raila, Ruto visit Uganda's high commissioner to Kenya at Nairobi Hospital

- The deputy president visited Phoebe Otala on Wednesday morning to wish her quick recovery

- Ruto's visit came after the former prime minister was also spotted at the health facility

- Otala was admitted at the hospital about two weeks ago following a car accident she was involved in

Deputy President William Ruto and Orange Democratic Movement (ODM) party leader Raila Odinga are among high ranking leaders who have visited Uganda's High Commissioner to Kenya Phoebe Otala at Nairobi Hospital.

It is understood the two politicians paid the Ugandan envoy a visit to wish her quick recovery after she was involved in a road accident which left her with a broken leg.

TUKO.co.ke on Thursday, June 20, learnt the DP was at Nairobi Hospital on Wednesday morning, a few days after the former prime minister's visit.

Uganda's New Vision indicated Otala had received a number of high profile visitors at the health facility since she was admitted in June 2019.

Otala was admitted after she suffered a broken left leg in a motor accident at Kimwanga on the Eldoret-Malaba highway.

She was reportedly flown to Nairobi by AMREF flying doctors and received at Wilson Airport by officials from Kenya's ministry of Foreign Affairs.

Uganda's embassy in Nairobi had confirmed she was in a stable condition.
