Harun Aydin: Turkish National Who Might Have Caused Cancellation of Ruto's Uganda Trip

  • William Ruto was blocked from flying to Uganda for allegedly not seeking clearance as all civil servants should
  • However, the inclusion of a Turkish national, Harun Aydin, in Ruto’s delegation might have raised eyebrows
  • Aydin has been frequenting Kenya and accompanied the DP in recent trips to Sudan and Zanzibar.
  • He was in 2001 arrested and charged in Germany, accused of planning violence as a member of a terrorist group

Deputy President William Ruto was on Monday, August 2, treated to a rude shock when he was blocked from travelling to Uganda for a private visit.

Ruto was blocked from flying to Kenya’s western neighbour for allegedly not seeking official clearance as all civil servants should.

His seven-man entourage, which included MPs Oscar Sudi, Ndindi Nyoro and Benjamin Tayari was allowed to proceed to Uganda.

Reports indicate that the DP was to fly privately using a Cessna 560XL and was all set to embark on his unknown mission in Entebbe before he was unceremoniously stopped while already at Wilson Airport.

He was reportedly asked by the immigration team at the airport to first seek clearance from the Head of Civil Service as is officially required.

Harun Aydin

But while the limelight was on the DP, it has emerged that the inclusion of a Turkish national, Harun Aydin, in Ruto’s delegation might have raised eyebrows and triggered the dramatic events.

The 54-year-old Turk, said to be a businessman, has in the past been linked to terrorism. According to The New York Times, Aydin was in October 2001 arrested in Germany and charged with “having planned serious acts of violence as a member of a terrorist group with an Islamic fundamentalist background”.

He was arrested as he was about to board an Iran Air flight to Tehran and was found with luggage containing camouflage clothing, a chemical weapons protection suit, a ski mask and materials to produce an explosive detonator.

It was alleged that Aydin, who was a student in Germany, was a leading member of the militant group based in Cologne known as the “Caliph of the Cologne” that wanted to unite the Islamic world in a single caliphate based in a liberated Istanbul.

He was also accused of giving instructions for serious acts of violence such as murder and manslaughter in several incidences.

Before the Frankfurt arrest, Aydin had been sentenced to four years in jail after he was found guilty of advocating the murder of a rival, Halil Ibrahim, but was later acquitted.

It is this dark image that has raised suspicion about his frequent visits to Kenya and close interaction with the Deputy President.

Aydin, who first arrived in the country in 2018, has made frequent visits to Kenya. His passport, No. U20470175, has been stamped more than five times at the Jomo Kenyatta International Airport this year alone.

He made reportedly made six return trips to Nairobi from Istanbul in 2018, four trips in 2019 and two last year.

The Turk accompanied Ruto to Sudan on July 5 and returned on July 7 and was also part of the delegation that travelled with the DP to Zanzibar on July 23 and returned on July 24 through Wilson Airport.

Aydin’s mission in the country remains unclear, and so are Ruto’s many trips across the region.

During last month’s trip to Uganda, the DP unveiled a vaccine manufacturing plant before holding a private meeting with Uganda’s President Yoweri Museveni, a meeting that caught Nairobi unawares as it was not officially planned.

After Monday's aborted trip, Ruto tweeted

“Isorait...Tumwachie Mungu” (It’s alright, let's leave it to God), even as his allies castigated the incident.
