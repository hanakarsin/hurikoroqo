Kenyan Newspapers Review for June 18: Ruto Narrows Down Search for Running Mate to Mt Kenya, Coast

The Kenyan newspapers on Friday, June 18, reported Deputy President William Ruto has narrowed down his search for a running mate to the vote-rich Mt Kenya and coast region.

The dailies also reported that ODM party leader Raila Odinga and his handshake partner President Uhuru Kenyatta have begun pre-election talks to form a coalition ahead of the 2022 General Election.

1. Daily Nation

Deputy President William Ruto has narrowed down his search for a running mate to the vote-rich Mt Kenya region and the coast.

This comes after his Western Kenya allies gave up in the push for the position.

Mumias East member of parliament Benjamin Washiali said the leaders from the region backing the DP had agreed to settle for other seats.

"Given the kind of support the DP has, he will definitely succeed President Uhuru Kenyatta. I don't think we will reach a point Western will force him to pick one of us as a deputy. We also do not count in terms of numbers so we cannot push for the position," Washiali said.

2. The Standard

President Uhuru Kenyatta and his political friend Raila Odinga have begun talks to enter into a formal pre-election agreement as the 2022 General Election approaches.

On Thursday, June 17, allies of the duo met at a Nairobi hotel to discuss details of a possible alliance that they said will help them capture power.

The meeting was attended by Jubilee secretary-general Raphael Tuju, Jubilee vice-chairman David Murathe, Deputy Majority-Whip Maoka Maore and Orange Democratic Movement secretary-general Edwin Sifuna.

The meeting came after Uhuru reportedly said he would back one National Super Alliance candidates to succeed him.

"It was a meeting of like minds to take to the next level a mechanism for a coalition agreement with ODM," Murathe said.

3. People Daily

The publication reported that over 40 members of parliament were restless after the Independent Electoral and Boundaries Commission (IEBC) said all leaders seeking to vie for elective posts in 2022 must have a degree.

Some of the MPs include Oscar Sudi (Kapseret), Kimani Ngujiri (Bahari), George Aladwa (Makadara), Junet Mohamed (Suna East), Imran Okoth (Kibra) and Dan Wanyama (Webuye West).

Others are Jayne Kihara (Naivasha), George Theuri (Embakasi West) and Abdullswamad Sheriff (Mvita).

Junet Mohamed, however, disputed the information on parliament's website showing he holds a diploma in business management from Woodvale college.

"I already did the degree a long time ago, in fact, I will call the clerk to correct my records," he said.

Deputy President William Ruto, however, said the requirement is irrational and would bar the electorate from electing leaders of their choice. He said leadership is God-given.

"The constitution prohibits any unreasonable restrictions being placed on the way of voters or candidates to be able to vie or vote in any elections," said Ruto.

4. Taifa Leo

The paper pointed fingers at the top political leaders in the country for aiding in the spread of the novel coronavirus during the Madaraka Day celebrations held in Kisumu.

The paper specifically blamed Uhuru, Raila and Ruto for the COVID-19 mess in Kisumu and other Nyanza counties whose cases have soared since the Madaraka fete was held in the city.

The government on Thursday, June 17, announced a 7pm to 4am curfew in Kisumu and 12 other counties from the country's western region after declaring them disease-infected counties.

Health Cabinet Secretary Mutahi Kagwe said the affected counties had registered a positivity rate of 21% against a national average of 9% over the last 14 days.
